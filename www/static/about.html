Pathway

About Pathway

Pathway is a project developed by Cornell Data Science in collaboration with the Future of Learning Lab in order to help students explore their interests and find related classes and majors.

Pathway uses the official course enrollment records kept by Cornell to build relationships, make recommendations, and generate graphs among classes. Pathway does not use data related to class times, availability, or prerequisite structure, and as such should not be considered a final planning tool. Recommendations from Pathway are suggestions that are intended to inspire and aid exploration, but always check that classes are available to you using the Course Roster before enrolling.

Pathway As A Research Project

Your Contribution

By using Pathway, you are volunteering for research. We record de-identified information about your interactions with Pathway. Your usage is tracked through a unique hashed ID that is assigned when you log in using your NetID credentials. We may also ask you to answer some simple survey questions. In our ongoing effort to improve the accuracy and usefulness of the service, you may be exposed to some variation in the presentation of information available to you on Pathway. You may stop participating in this research at any time by no longer using Pathway. Neither your academic progress nor program eligibility is contingent on your participation or non-participation in Pathway. We study the data we gather through Pathway by analyzing trends and patterns in search and interaction. We also examine how search patterns on Pathway are related to course and major choice by connecting Pathway records to official registrar records. Because the data we collect is de-identified, we cannot and will not identify you by name in any discussions or publications, nor describe data in such a way that you could be identified.

Our Commitment

We use the data we gather to continually improve Pathway and contribute to an ongoing discussion of educational improvement at Cornell and other institutions. In the process of improving your user experience, we explore and analyze aggregated user data.
